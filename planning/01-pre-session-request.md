# Pre-Session Materials Request — Marketing Team

## Purpose

Before our discovery workshop, we need to review everything the Marketing team already has documented. This prevents us from spending session time on things already written down — and ensures the workshop only covers gaps.

---

## What to Send to the Marketing Team

**Subject:** Vitorra Website Rebuild — Materials Request Before Our Workshop

---

Hi [Marketing Team Lead],

As we prepare for the website rebuild workshop, I need to review any materials your team has already produced before we meet. This will make our session far more focused and efficient — we'll only need to discuss what isn't already captured.

Please share anything you have in any of the following areas:

**Brand & Identity**
- Brand guidelines (colours, fonts, logo rules)
- Brand positioning statements or taglines
- Tone of voice documents

**Customer & Audience**
- Customer research reports or surveys
- Customer persona documents or profiles
- Audience segmentation work

**Products**
- Product one-pagers, brochures, or datasheets (for FET, SEAL, Coffee, Logistics)
- Product pricing sheets
- Certifications, lab results, or third-party validations
- Technical specifications

**Sales & Pipeline**
- Sales process documentation
- Pitch decks or sales presentations
- Distributor or partner materials

**Competitors**
- Any competitor analysis documents
- Market positioning work

**Digital / Marketing**
- Social media analytics reports
- Website traffic data (if any)
- Email campaign reports

**You don't need to prepare anything new — send whatever already exists, even if it's rough or informal.** A WhatsApp voice note, a Google Doc draft, a PDF brochure — whatever you have is useful.

Please send everything to [john@vitorra.org] by [DATE — set 3 days before the workshop].

I'll review it all before we meet so we can hit the ground running in the session.

Thank you,
John

---

## After Receiving the Materials

Before the workshop, John should:

1. Read everything received
2. Mark which of the 55 workshop questions are already answered by the materials
3. Note which answers are partial and need probing
4. Mark which questions are fully unanswered and need dedicated time
5. Reorder the workshop agenda to prioritise the unanswered areas

This turns a 4-hour general session into a targeted conversation on what actually matters.
